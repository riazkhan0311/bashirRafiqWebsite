About Bashir Rafiq

Bashir Rafiq was born in 1931 in Pakistan. He graduated from the University of the Punjab in 1953 and then undertook a five year course at the Missionary Training College, Rabwah, Pakistan.

In 1959, he was assigned to the London Mosque as missionary and deputy Imam. In 1964, he was appointed Imam of the London Mosque.

Bashir Rafiq is the author of 12 books on Islam and Contemporary Religion. He was editor of 2 English magazines, The Review of Religions and The Muslim Herald. He was also on the Editorial Board of the Islamic Magazine Al Furqan.

Bashir Rafiq was the convener of an "International Conference on the Deliverance of Jesus from the Cross" held at the Commonwealth Institute, Kensington, London in June 1978. Rafiq was invited by President William Tubman of Liberia, as a state guest in 1968. He was decorated with the titular honour of Liberian Chief.

He passed away on October 11th 2016. Inna Lillahi Wa Inna Ilayhi Raaji'oon
